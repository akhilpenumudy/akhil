About Me

My research interests lie at the intersection of machine learning, chemical engineering, and molecular sensing technologies.

I have experience working in corperate R&D (i.e., Merck), where I've applied machine learning and data engineering to solve real-world problems in both drug discovery and pharmaceutical manufacturing.

Throughout my academic journey, I've been passionate about applying computational methods to solve complex molecular engineering problems. I believe that the integration of machine learning and traditional Biomolecular engineering approaches can lead to significant breakthroughs in areas such as drug discovery, materials science, and environmental monitoring.

When I'm not in the lab or coding, I enjoy exploring new technologies, contributing to open-source projects, and tutoring/mentoring younger students in STEM subjects.

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning for Molecular Sciences
  • Computational Chemistry and Cheminformatics
  • Biosensors and Volatile Organic Compound (VOC) Detection
  • Computational Biology and Bioinformatics
  • Sensor Technology and Data Analysis
