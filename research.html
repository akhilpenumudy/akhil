Research Methodology

My research integrates computational chemistry, machine learning, and computer vision to accelerate the discovery and development of novel molecular sensors and bioactive compounds:

  1. Data Acquisition: Extracting and curating chemical and spectral data from literature, experiments, and databases.
  2. Feature Engineering: Encoding molecular structures using SMILES, graph embeddings, and cheminformatics descriptors.
  3. Model Development: Applying Random Forests, SVMs, deep neural networks, and GNNs to model chemical reactivity and interactions.
  4. Benchmarking: Comparing classical machine learning approaches with emerging quantum computing algorithms.
  5. Experimental Validation: Collaborating with wet-lab teams to validate computational predictions on real sensor systems.

Research Impact

This work advances the intersection of artificial intelligence and molecular science by creating scalable and interpretable models for molecular detection and drug discovery. Applications span environmental monitoring, agricultural diagnostics, and pharmaceutical design — bridging the gap between computational prediction and experimental realization.
