Research Methodology

Our research employs a multi-faceted approach to volatile organic compound (VOC) detection and classification:

  1. Data Collection: Gathering sensor readings from various VOC interactions with colorimetric dye arrays
  2. Feature Engineering: Extracting meaningful features from molecular structures using SMILES representations and cheminformatics techniques
  3. Model Development: Training and optimizing machine learning models including Random Forests, SVMs, and neural networks
  4. Validation: Rigorous testing against known compounds and novel VOCs to ensure generalizability
  5. Interface Development: Creating user-friendly interfaces for non-technical users to interact with the models

Research Impact

This research has significant implications for environmental monitoring, agricultural pest detection, and medical diagnostics. By creating more accurate and accessible VOC detection systems, we aim to enable early detection of plant diseases, improve indoor air quality monitoring, and potentially assist in disease diagnosis through breath analysis.
